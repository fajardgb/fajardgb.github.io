About Me

Hi! My name is Gabriel Fajardo, and I am the lab manager in the Social Cognitive and Neural Sciences Lab at Columbia University (PI: Jon Freeman). My research interests are at the intersection of social psychology and cognitive neuroscience, with an emphasis on computational approaches, or Computational Social Neuroscience.

While machines have achieved superhuman performance in many domains including chess, medical image diagnostics, and driving, they still have a long way to go in understanding the complex social world. I aim to study the mechanisms through which the social brain efficiently and effortlessly encodes the rich, dynamic, and high-dimensional data from the social world. Additionally, I am interested in how the brain's processing of social information differs from non-social information.

I was born in Lima, Peru and I moved to a town next to Princeton, NJ when I was 6 years old. I graduated from Boston College in Spring 2023, where I worked in the Social and Cognitive Computational Neuroscience Lab with Stefano Anzellotti. My research at BC was on utilizing multivariate statistical dependence analyses based on neural networks (MVPN) to identify brain regions responsible for audio-visual integration.

Outside of research, I enjoy soccer, films, and exploring NYC!
